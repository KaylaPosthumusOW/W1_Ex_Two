Cockatiel

Cockatiels are small, slender cockatoos with a distinctive crest and orange cheeks. They are native to Australia, where they are also called weiros or quarrians.

Fun facts about these birds

  1. Cockatiels were discovered by Europeans in Australia in 1770.
  2. Cockatiels enter their nest hollow tail first, their tail being so long that they cannot turn around in the nest.
  3. Cockatiels do not make a screeching sound like many other parrots and can learn to 'speak'.
